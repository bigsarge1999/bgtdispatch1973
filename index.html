Legal & Licensing Reference (Florida)

Researched from Florida agency sources for Okaloosa County — verify everything directly before relying on it

Read this first: this section is a starting-point reference, not legal advice. Requirements change, agencies interpret rules case-by-case, and nothing here should be treated as final. Confirm everything directly with FLHSMV, the Florida Department of Revenue, and a licensed Florida attorney before operating.

RequirementWhy it likely appliesAgency
Salvage Motor Vehicle Dealer (SD) License Florida Statute 320.27 defines a "salvage motor vehicle dealer" as anyone who acquires salvaged or wrecked motor vehicles for the purpose of reselling them or their parts. If you personally buy wrecked or parted-out bikes to break down and resell components — as opposed to buying already-removed individual parts from someone else — this license very likely applies to you. FLHSMV, Bureau of Dealer Services
LLC State Registration Required to operate as an LLC in Florida FL Division of Corporations (Sunbiz)
EIN Needed for business banking and tax filing IRS
Sales Tax / Reseller Certificate Required to collect and remit Florida sales tax, and to buy inventory tax-free for resale Florida Department of Revenue
Secondhand Dealer Registration (Ch. 538, F.S.) Possibly applicable depending on how used parts are classified under this chapter — genuinely unclear from public guidance alone; worth a direct call to confirm Florida Department of Revenue
Local Business Tax Receipt Standard local occupational license for operating a business in the county Okaloosa County Tax Collector

The physical-location issue is the one to resolve first. Florida's SD license application process (form HSMV 86056) generally requires a permanent, established place of business — an enclosed building with at least 100 sq ft of dedicated office space, a permanent sign visible from the road, and space set aside for the business that isn't shared with other uses. It is not clearly available for a purely home/garage-based operation. Before assuming this business can run entirely out of a home garage, call FLHSMV directly and ask how they treat a parts-only operation like this one — the answer materially affects whether this stays home-based or needs a small commercial space.

Salvage dealers are exempt from the garage liability insurance requirement that applies to some other dealer types, but a surety bond and other application requirements may still apply — confirm current figures directly with FLHSMV rather than relying on this note, since bond costs and thresholds are set by the state and can change.

Recordkeeping: Florida requires dealers to keep purchase, sale, and title records for five years. Regardless of which license ultimately applies, keeping detailed records of where every part came from (already built into the Add Part form's Source field) is good practice and likely to be expected by any of the agencies above.

General, non-Florida-specific items worth knowing about:

Trademark: reselling genuine used OEM Harley-Davidson parts is generally protected under the first-sale doctrine, but branding and marketing should avoid implying official dealer or manufacturer affiliation.

Shipping: engines and other parts with residual fuel or attached batteries may fall under hazmat shipping rules — check with your carrier before listing anything that hasn't been fully drained and prepped.

Environmental: draining fluids (oil, coolant, brake fluid) from parts before resale may trigger state or local hazardous-waste disposal rules — check with the Florida Department of Environmental Protection or your county before disposing of anything down a drain or in regular trash.
